Speech Watermarking with Discrete Intermediate Representations

Anonymous authors
Paper under double-blind review

Abstract

Speech watermarking techniques can proactively mitigate the potential harms of instant voice cloning techniques by embedding signals into speech that are invisible to humans but algorithmically detectable. Previous approaches typically embed watermark messages into the continuous space, while this paper propose DiscreteWM, a speech watermarking framework that hides watermarks into the discrete intermediate representations of speech. Specifically, we map speech into discrete latent space and hide watermarks by changing the modular arithmetic relation of discrete IDs. To ensure the imperceptibility of watermarks, we also propose a manipulator model to select the candidate tokens for reconstruction. Experimental results demonstrate that our framework surpasses the continuous counterparts in voice cloning detection and steganography tasks in terms of imperceptibility and robustness. Furthermore, DiscreteWM can encode 1 to 150 bits of watermark within a 1-second speech clip, allowing for a flexible inference strategy in various scenarios.
